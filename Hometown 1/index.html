Discover the History

Rome, the Eternal City, has over 2,500 years of history! Founded in 753 BCE by the legendary twins Romulus and Remus, it started as a kingdom before becoming a powerful Republic in 509 BCE. Rome expanded its influence across the Mediterranean after the Punic Wars.

In 27 BCE, it became an Empire under Augustus Caesar, kicking off the Pax Romana – a golden age of peace and iconic landmarks like the Colosseum and Pantheon. Though the Western Roman Empire fell in 476 CE, the Eastern half (Byzantine Empire) thrived.

During the Renaissance, Rome became an artistic and cultural hub, home to masterpieces by Michelangelo. Today, it’s a vibrant blend of ancient history and modern life, enchanting visitors from around the world!

Top Attractions

Rome, the Eternal City, is home to iconic monuments that showcase its rich history and culture. The Colosseum and Roman Forum reflect the grandeur of ancient Rome, while the Pantheon amazes with its timeless architecture. In Vatican City, St. Peter’s Basilica and the Sistine Chapel highlight Renaissance artistry. The Trevi Fountain enchants visitors with its legend, and Castel Sant’Angelo offers stunning views of the city. From the Altare della Patria to the Baths of Caracalla, each monument tells a story of Rome’s enduring legacy.

Roman Cuisine

Pizza: Rome’s version of pizza is thin and crispy, called **Pizza al Taglio**, often served in squares. It’s a street food favorite, topped with simple ingredients like fresh tomatoes, mozzarella, and olive oil.

Carbonara: This Roman specialty was born in the city! Made with **pecorino cheese**, eggs, pancetta, and black pepper, it’s a creamy, comforting dish with no cream needed—just tradition and flavor.

Gelato: Rome’s **gelato** is a must-try! Unlike regular ice cream, gelato has less air and fat, making it creamier and more intense in flavor. Popular spots like **Giolitti** have been serving gelato for over 100 years.
